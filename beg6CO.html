Algorithms: 7

Once we have achieved the EO-Ledge, the next step is to orient the 6 remaining corners. In the beginner version, we break this down into two phases. First, we intuitively put oriented corners in the D layer using R2 {U} R2 triggers (occasionally there will be less than two oriented corners on the entire cube, so we may need to do a random Sune algorithm to get two oriented corners). This will leave only 4 corners remaining to be oriented, all in the U layer. These are the 7 familiar OCLLs, and CFOP algorithms for these cases can be used. However, utilising the greater freedom, these are shorter and faster algorithms.
